Abstract

Strain NoAHT (=KACC 23135T=JCM 35999T), a novel Gram-negative, motile bacterium with a rod-shaped morphology, was isolated from the zoo animal faecal samples, specifically the long-tailed goral species Naemorhedus caudatus. The novel bacterial strain grew optimally in a nutrient broth medium under the following conditions: 1–2% (w/v) NaCl, pH 7–8 and 30 °C. The strain NoAHT exhibited high tolerance to NaCl, with the ability to tolerate up to 7% (w/v) NaCl. Based on phylogenetic analyses using 16S rRNA gene sequencing, strain NoAHT was found to have the closest relatedness to Comamonas jiangduensis YW1T (98.5%), Comamonas aquatica ATCC 11330T (97.9%), Comamonas resistens KCTC 82561T (97.9%), Comamonas fluminis CJ34T (97.7%) and Comamonas suwonensis EJ-4T (97.6%). The genome size and genomic DNA G+C content of strain NoAHT were 4.05 Mbp and 55.9 mol%, respectively. A whole-genome-level comparison of strain NoAHT with C. jiangduensis YWT, Comamonas kerstersii LMG 3475T, C. aquatica NBRC 14918T, Comamonas terrigena NBRC 12685T and C. fluminis CJ34T revealed the following orthologous average nucleotide identity values: 80.1, 79.0, 78.6, 76.3 and 75.2%, respectively. The major polar lipids of strain NoAHT were phosphatidylethanolamine, phosphatidylglycerol and diphosphatidylglycerol. Considering our findings in chemotaxonomic, genotypic and phenotypic characteristics, strain NoAHT is identified as a novel species within the genus Comamonas, for which the name Comamonas halotolerans sp. nov. is proposed.

The genus Comamonas was first described by De Vos et al. [1], with the type species being Comamonas terrigena [1]. The genus Comamonas consists of Gram-stain-negative bacteria classified under the family Comamonadaceae within the phylum Pseudomonadota. Most members of the genus Comamonas are rod-shaped, non-spore-forming, catalase- and oxidase-positive bacteria that primarily use ubiquinone Q-8 as their main respiratory isoprenoid quinone [2–4]. As of July 2024, the genus Comamonas comprises 28 species with validly published and correct names, as documented in the List of Prokaryotic names with Standing in Nomenclature taxonomy browser database (https://lpsn.dsmz.de/genus/comamonas). The complete genomes of Comamonas deposited in the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI) databases show a range of genome sizes among the assemblies from type material, from 3.3 Mbp (e.g. Comamonas flocculans NLF-7–7) to 5.3 Mbp (e.g. Comamonas resistens ZM22). The genus Comamonas is found in a diverse range of habitats across various environments, such as freshwater (Comamonas aquatilis SB30-Chr27-3T and Comamonas aquatica NBRC 14918T), sediment (Comamonas jiangduensis YW1T, Comamonas sediminis S3T, Comamonas zonglianii BF-3T and Comamonas terrae A3-3T), activated sludge (Comamonas badia 12T, Comamonas denitrificans 123T and C. resistens KCTC 82561T), rockfish (Comamonas piscis CNT), insects (Comamonas odontotermitis Dant 3-8T) and food waste (Comamonas composti BCRC 17659T) [2–13]. Recent findings have identified Comamonas species in the faecal microbiome of various animals (e.g. mice, piglets and hens), highlighting the necessity for further investigation due to its notable antibiotic resistance, which allows it to act as an opportunistic pathogen by dominating faecal communities after antibiotic treatment [14–18]. In this study, we described a novel bacterial species, designated as strain NoAHT, isolated from the faeces of zoo animals, specifically the long-tailed goral (Naemorhedus caudatus) at Seoul Grand Park.

Isolation and ecology

Strain NoAHT was isolated from N. caudatus faeces at Seoul Grand Park, located at coordinates 37° 25′ 39.1″ N, 127° 1′ 1.3″ E. The strain NoAHT was isolated in our previous study [14]. Briefly, the animal faecal sample from N. caudatus was promptly used for bacterial screening, diluted in PBS and spread onto Reasoner’s 2A (R2A) agar plates containing 0.5 g l−1 yeast extract, proteose peptone No. 3, casamino acid, d-glucose and soluble starch; 0.3 g l−1 sodium pyruvate, K2HPO4; 0.05 g l−1 MgSO4; and 15 g l−1 agar powder. The colonies were chosen, subjected to a purification process involving repeated streaking and then incubated at 30 °C for 48 h. For the long-term preservation of the bacterial strain NoAHT, it was cultured in nutrient broth (NB) liquid medium, mixed with an equal volume (1:1 ratio) of 100% (v/v) glycerol and then stored at −80 °C. For comparative physiology and taxonomic analyses, the reference type strains C. jiangduensis YW1T (=KACC 16697T) and C. aquatica NBRC 14918T (=KACC 11937T) were selected and obtained from the Korean Agricultural Culture Collection (KACC) in the Republic of Korea.

The 16S rRNA gene sequence of strain NoAHT was amplified using PCR with the universal primers 27F (5′-AGA GTT TGA TCM TGG CTC AG-3′) and 1492R (5′-TAC GGY TAC CTT GTT ACG ACT T-3′), followed by purification and sequencing of the PCR products by Macrogen (Republic of Korea) [19]. For phylogenetic identification of strain NoAHT, its nearly full-length 16S rRNA gene sequence (1366 bp) was compared with sequences from Comamonas species obtained from both the NCBI (https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/nuccore) and EzBioCloud (http://www.ezbiocloud.net/identify) databases. The 16S rRNA gene sequence of strain NoAHT showed similarity to species within the Comamonas genus, including C. jiangduensis YW1T (=KACC 16697T, 98.5% similarity) and C. aquatica NBRC 14918T (=KACC 11937T, 97.9% similarity), as identified in the NCBI database. Multiple sequence alignments and the reconstruction of phylogenetic trees were performed based on 16S rRNA gene sequences using the maximum-likelihood, neighbour-joining (NJ) and minimum-evolution algorithms available in the Molecular Evolutionary Genetics Analysis (mega) software version X (http://www.megasoftware.net) [20]. Additionally, the trees were evaluated using a bootstrap test with 1000 replications and the Kimura two-parameter model. A total of 29 type strains were analysed for their 16S rRNA sequences, with Burkholderia cepacia ATCC 25416T used as an outgroup (Figs 1 and S1, available in the online Supplementary Material). The top five strains with the highest similarity to strain NoAHT were C. jiangduensis YW1T (98.5%), C. aquatica ATCC 11330T (97.9%), C. resistens KCTC 82561T (97.9%), C. fluminis CJ34T (97.7%) and Comamonas suwonensis EJ-4T (97.6%) (Table 1). Further analysis selected the reference strains C. jiangduensis YW1T and C. aquatica NBRC 14918T due to the similarity of their 16S rRNA gene sequences and phylogenetic trees.

The draft genome sequences of strain NoAHT and C. jiangduensis YW1T were analysed using the Illumina NovaSeq 6000 sequencer (Illumina) and assembled with Unicycler version 0.4.7, with sequencing and assembly procedures conducted by DNA Link (Republic of Korea). The CheckM program v1.2.2 was used to analyse the genome contamination rates of strain NoAHT and C. jiangduensis YW1T [21]. The draft genome of strain NoAHT and strain YW1T was deposited in the GenBank database with the accession numbers JASAUZ000000000 and JARXOV000000000, respectively. Gene prediction and annotation outcomes were examined using the web-based tool DFAST (https://dfast.ddbj.nig.ac.jp/dfc/) v1.2.0 [22]. Several analyses were performed to evaluate the genome similarities between strain NoAHT and 18 other publicly available type strains with whole-genome sequences. The average nucleotide identity (ANI) was calculated using two different tools: the OrthoANIu algorithm available at EZBioCloud (https://www.ezbiocloud.net/tools/ani) and the blast-based JSpeciesWS ANIb tool (http://jspecies.ribohost.com/jspeciesws) [23–25]. Additionally, digital DNA–DNA hybridization (dDDH) was assessed using the genome-to-genome distance calculator 3.0 (https://ggdc.dsmz.de/ggdc.php) [26]. To further substantiate the novelty of strain NoAHT within the genus Comamonas, as indicated by the OrthoANI, ANIb and dDDH results, a phylogenomic tree based on whole-genome sequences was reconstructed using the type strain genome server (https://tygs.dsmz.de/) and visualized with the Interactive Tree of Life tool version 6.9.1 (https://itol.embl.de/) [27, 28]. Moreover, a phylogenomic tree based on 81 core genes was constructed using the up-to-date bacterial core gene set version 2 [29]. To identify the distinctive features of strain NoAHT, its assembled genome was annotated using the Rapid Annotation with Subsystem Technology (RAST) tool (https://rast.nmpdr.org/rast.cgi) and BlastKOALA web tool version 3.0 (https://www.kegg.jp/blastkoala/) [30, 31]. To investigate the antibiotic resistance genes (ARGs) in the genome of strain NoAHT, ARG and virulence factor profiling was conducted using CLC Genomics Workbench version 22 (Qiagen), based on four databases: ResFinder nucleotide database version 4.1, the CARD nucleotide database (2021-updated), the QMI-AR nucleotide database (2021-updated) and the VFDB nucleotide database (2019-updated).

The Gram type of strain NoAHT was identified using a Gram-staining kit (bioMérieux) in accordance with the manufacturer’s instructions. Cell size and morphology were assessed using phase-contrast microscopy (Carl Zeiss) and a Quanta 250 FEG field-emission scanning electron microscope (FEI, USA). To determine the optimal growth conditions for strain NoAHT, cell growth was monitored under varying temperatures (4–46 °C in 2 °C increments), NaCl concentrations (0–8% in 1% increments) and pH levels (4–12 in 1 pH increments) for 3 days at 30 °C in NB medium. After sterilizing the medium at 121 °C for 20 min, the pH was adjusted with 1 M filtered HCl or KOH. The growth of strain NoAHT was then assessed on various media, including NB (BD Difco), Luria–Bertani (LB) agar (BioShop), R2A agar, tryptic soy broth (TSB) (BD Difco) and McConkey medium (BD Difco), all of which were incubated at 30 °C for 3 days. Anaerobic growth experiments for strain NoAHT were conducted using NB liquid medium (CO2:N2=8:2) at 30 °C for 2 weeks, with simultaneous tests employing Pseudomonas putida KT2440 as the negative control and Escherichia coli ATCC 25922 as the positive control. Bacterial cell swimming (on 0.3% agar) and swarming (on 0.5% agar) motilities were tested on NB agar plates at 30 °C for 3 days. Catalase and oxidase activities were assessed by introducing 10 µl of 3% hydrogen peroxide and 1% Wurster’s reagent into cultures of strain NoAHT and the reference strains (C. jiangduensis YW1T and C. aquatica NBRC 14918T). Additionally, the strain’s ability to hydrolyse starch, cellulose and 1% skimmed milk was assessed using NB medium. To determine the enzymatic and biochemical characteristics of strain NoAHT and the reference type strains (C. jiangduensis YW1T and C. aquatica NBRC 14918T), the API 20NE and API ZYM kits (bioMérieux) were used. Cells were suspended in a 0.85% NaCl solution to assess their physiological properties. Reactions were confirmed according to the manufacturer’s instructions: API 20NE readings were taken after 24 and 48 h, while API ZYM readings were taken after 4 h. To analyse quinones and polar lipids, the strain NoAHT was cultured in NB broth at 30 °C for 24 h with aeration (180 r.p.m.). An HPLC system, fitted with a Nova-Pack C18 reverse-phase column (4 µm particle size, Waters), was used for quinone analysis, eluting the quinones with a methanol/isopropyl ether solution (3:1, v/v) at a flow rate of 0.7 ml min−1 [32–34]. Polar lipids were analysed using a two-dimensional TLC technique, which involved separating the samples on TLC Kiesel gel 60 F254 plates (10×10 cm). The samples, prepared as described in previous studies, were first separated using a mobile phase of chloroform, methanol and deionized water (DW) (65:25:3.8, v/v) in the initial direction, followed by further separation in the second direction with a mobile phase of chloroform, acetic acid, methanol and DW (80:12:15:3.6, v/v) [35–37].

Genome features

The draft genome of strain NoAHT, with an estimated length of 4 053 928 base pairs and a G+C content of 55.9 mol%, is distributed across 74 contigs, where the N50 size of 282 051 bp signifies that half of the genome is encompassed within contigs of this size or larger. For C. jiangduensis YW1T, the drafted genome consisted of a total of 67 contigs, with a cumulative length of 3 584 245 bp and a G+C content of 58.9 mol%. The genome contamination rates indicated low contamination levels for both strains (strains NoAHT and YW1T), with values of 3.07 and 2.64%, respectively, which fall below the standard recommendation of 5% for classifying a genome as contaminated. The gene prediction and annotation process identified a total of 3657 protein-coding genes, 3 rRNA genes and 74 tRNA genes in the genome of strain NoAHT. Additionally, the gene prediction and annotation process identified a total of 3298 protein-encoding genes, 3 rRNA genes and 73 tRNA genes in the genome of strain YW1T. Table 1 outlines the genomic characteristics of the novel strain NoAHT along with those of closely related type strains. Strain NoAHT was identified as a novel species within the Comamonas genus, meeting the ANI and dDDH cutoff values of 95 and 70%, respectively, which indicates its distinctiveness from the other analysed reference type strains. The ANI values between strain NoAHT and its related species were below the 95% threshold, ranging from 71.9 to 80.1% (OrthoANI) and 70.6 to 79.3% (ANIb), confirming its distinctiveness. The dDDH values between strain NoAHT and the 18 type species were below the 70% threshold, ranging from 13.6 to 28.3%. The analysis of the phylogenomic tree and core-gene phylogenetic tree provided additional evidence, confirming that strain NoAHT is indeed a novel species within the genus Comamonas, consistent with the findings presented in Table 1(Figs S2 and S3). Based on the RAST annotation, 57 genes related to motility and the flagellar system, including the flg, flh, fli and fla operons, were identified, which aligns with the results of the motility experiment. In total, 50 genes associated with stress responses, including osmotic stress, were identified, such as aquaporin Z and the glycerol uptake facilitator protein. The aquaporin water channel selectively facilitates the movement of water across the cell membrane, helping to regulate cellular pressure in response to osmotic stress [38]. Additionally, the glycerol uptake facilitator protein enables the uptake of glycerol to maintain internal homeostasis, suggesting that the halotolerant strain NoAHT can effectively regulate osmotic pressure under varying external osmotic conditions [39]. Analyses using the BlastKOALA web tool also confirmed the presence of glycine betaine/proline (ProX, ProW and ProV), suggesting that strain NoAHT accumulates compatible solutes like glycine betaine and proline to counteract high osmolarity and prevent cellular water loss [40, 41]. In the metagenomes of faecal samples from various animals, including the long-tailed goral, the presence of Comamonas species has been reported, and its relative abundance significantly increased to account for more than half of the community when treated with an antibiotic cocktail containing ampicillin, neomycin, metronidazole and vancomycin [14–18]. This suggests that the novel Comamonas sp. NoAHT may exhibit strong antibiotic resistance and highlights the need for further investigation into its role in the gut microbiome. The genome of strain NoAHT was found to contain six genes (blaPAU-1, formA, fosL, smeE, mexB, oprM) that can confer antibiotic resistance, with the minimum identity and length set at 50%. Given that Comamonas species is known to rarely act as an opportunistic pathogen, an investigation of virulence factors revealed 31 virulence factors in the genome of strain NoAHT, including flagellar and pilus assembly genes (flgG, fli, pil, motA), a chemotaxis signalling system (cheWY) and stress tolerance genes (katA for oxidative stress, clpP for phagosome stress and ure for acidity stress) [42]. This suggests that novel sp. NoAHT might be a highly antibiotic-resistant pathogen with virulence factors present in the intestinal tracts of animals.

Microscopic analysis indicated that the cell size of strain NoAHT was 0.4–0.5×1.0–1.8 µm. During the exponential phase of growth in NB medium at 30 °C, strain NoAHT was identified as rod-shaped (Fig. S4). The optimal growth conditions for strain NoAHT were determined as follows: temperatures ranging from 8 to 42 °C (in 2 °C increments), with an optimum at 30 °C; NaCl concentrations ranging from 0 to 7% (in 1% increments), with an optimum at 1–2%; and pH levels ranging from 5 to 10 (in 1-unit increments), with an optimum between pH 7 and 8 (Table 2). Growth investigation across various media showed that strain NoAHT grew on NB, LB, R2A and TSB agar but exhibited only weak growth on MacConkey agar. In contrast, strains YW1T and NBRC 14918T demonstrated good growth on all tested media. Anaerobic growth was not observed in strain NoAHT or in most other previously reported members of the genus Comamonas [3, 4, 6]. Additionally, strain NoAHT exhibited both swimming and swarming motility. The presence of catalase and oxidase was confirmed by observing bubble formation for catalase activity and violet disc colouration for oxidase activity, with both strain NoAHT and the reference strains testing positive for both activities. According to API assay results, most of the reference type strains (C. jiangduensis YW1T, C. aquatica NBRC 14918 T and C. resistens KCTC 82561T) lacked the ability to hydrolyse 4-nitrophenyl-β-d-galactopyranoside, d-glucose, l-arabinose, d-mannose, N-acetyl-glucosamine, d-maltose and potassium gluconate. In contrast, strain NoAHT was able to hydrolyse all of these substrates (Table 2). The ability of strain NoAHT to hydrolyse these substrates aligns well with the characteristics of micro-organisms isolated from the intestinal or faecal environments of animals. The metabolic potential of strain NoAHT is particularly relevant to fibre degradation (e.g. l-arabinose), carbohydrate metabolism (e.g. d-glucose and d-maltose) and the utilization of mucosal substances (e.g. N-acetyl-glucosamine), which are essential activities in the gut environment [43–45]. The phenotypic characteristics of strain NoAHT and the reference strains are summarized in Table 2. For quinone analysis, the major respiratory quinone identified in strain NoAHT was ubiquinone-8 (Q-8), which is a characteristic feature of the genus Comamonas [4, 11]. The polar lipids of strain NoAHT were identified as phosphatidylethanolamine (PE), phosphatidylglycerol (PG) and diphosphatidylglycerol (DPG) (Fig. S5), exhibiting a distribution pattern similar to that of other Comamonas species [5, 46, 47]. Based on these results, the strain NoAHT represents a novel species of the genus Comamonas, for which the name Comamonas halotolerans sp. nov. is proposed.

Description of Comamonas halotolerans sp. nov.

Comamonas halotolerans (ha.lo.to’le.rans. Gr. masc. n. hals, salt; L. pres. part. tolerans, tolerating; N.L. part. adj. halotolerans, tolerating salt).

The bacterial cells were obligate aerobic, Gram-stain-negative and rod-shaped (0.4–0.5×1.0–1.8 µm) with positive catalase and oxidase activity. Cellular growth in NB medium was observed at 8–42 °C (optimum, 30 °C) and pH 5–10 (optimum, pH 7–8) with 0–7% (w/v) NaCl (optimum, 1–2%). The cells can reduce nitrates to nitrites and hydrolyse l-arginine, aesculin, 4-nitrophenyl-β-d-galactopyranoside, d-glucose, l-arabinose, d-mannose, d-mannitol, N-acetyl-glucosamine, d-maltose, potassium gluconate, adipic acid and malic acid; however, they could not hydrolyse cellulose, skimmed milk, starch, urea, gelatin and capric acid. Furthermore, the enzyme activities of alkaline phosphatase, valine arylamidase, acid phosphatase, β-glucuronidase, leucine arylamidase and Naphtol-AS-BI-phosphohydrolase were detected in strain NoAHT. In contrast, esterase (C4), esterase lipase (C8), α-chymotrypsin, α-fucosidase, l-tryptophane, trisodium citrate, phenylacetic acid, myristate lipase (C14), crystine arylamidase, trypsin, α-galactosidase, β-glucosidase, α-glucosidase, N-acetyl-β-glucosaminidase and α-mannosidase were not identified. Ubiquinone-8 (Q-8) was identified as the main respiratory quinone of strain NoAHT, with the major polar lipids being PE, PG and DPG.

The type strain NoAHT (=KACC 23135T=JCM 35999T) was isolated from an animal faecal sample (long-tailed goral) obtained from a local zoo in Seoul, Republic of Korea. The NoAHT strain has a genome size of 4.05 Mbp with a G+C content of 55.9 mol%. The GenBank accession numbers of the 16S rRNA and genome sequences of the NoAHT strain are OQ268077 and JASAUZ000000000, respectively.
